StubHub

**StubHub** is an American [ticket resale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticket_resale "Ticket resale") broker and a [primary ticket outlet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_ticket_outlet "Primary ticket outlet"). It is a subsidiary of StubHub Holdings, which also owns [Viagogo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viagogo "Viagogo").

StubHub was founded in 2000 by [Eric Baker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Baker_(businessman) "Eric Baker (businessman)") and [Jeff Fluhr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Fluhr "Jeff Fluhr") in [San Francisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco "San Francisco"). It was sold to [eBay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EBay "EBay") in 2007 for US$310 million. Viagogo, led by Baker, repurchased StubHub in 2020 for approximately US$4 billion, forming Stubhub Holdings, which became a public company in 2025.

StubHub has partnerships with professional sports leagues and companies including [Major League Baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Baseball "Major League Baseball") and [Anschutz Entertainment Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anschutz_Entertainment_Group "Anschutz Entertainment Group"). It was the first company to purchase an advertisement patch on an [NBA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NBA "NBA") jersey. The company was a subject of investigations into [ticket scalping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticket_scalping "Ticket scalping") by the United Kingdom's [Competition and Markets Authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Competition_and_Markets_Authority "Competition and Markets Authority").

## History

### Founding and early years

[Eric Baker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Baker_(businessman) "Eric Baker (businessman)") had the idea for an online secondhand event ticket marketplace after he struggled to purchase secondhand tickets for a showing of _[The Lion King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lion_King_(musical) "The Lion King (musical)")_ on [Broadway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadway_theatre "Broadway theatre").[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT1-1) He began developing the concept with [Jeff Fluhr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Fluhr "Jeff Fluhr") as part of a competition while both were students at [Stanford Graduate School of Business](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_Graduate_School_of_Business "Stanford Graduate School of Business").[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CBS1-2) In March 2000, the pair [incorporated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incorporation_(business) "Incorporation (business)") StubHub in [San Francisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco "San Francisco") to act as that marketplace. The company raised US$600,000 in [seed funding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seed_funding "Seed funding") by August 2000. Fluhr left school to be [chief executive officer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_executive_officer "Chief executive officer") of StubHub; Baker graduated in 2001 and became company president.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNN1-3) That year, StubHub signed its first deal with a professional sports team, partnering with the [Seattle Mariners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle_Mariners "Seattle Mariners").[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ESPN1-4) In 2002, eBay was in talks to acquire StubHub for US$20 million, although the agreement had later "fallen apart over price."[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-5) By its third year of operation, StubHub had 60 employees.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNN1-3) The company was incorporated on December 17, 2004 as Pugnacious Endeavors.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-6)

Fluhr described a 2004 falling out between himself and Baker over the company direction. Baker wanted to develop partnerships with sports leagues while Fluhr preferred to focus on building the StubHub brand. The difference in direction led to Baker leaving the company while retaining a 10 percent ownership stake.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT1-1)[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CBS1-2)[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNN1-3)[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-MW1-7) Baker founded competing agency [Viagogo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viagogo "Viagogo") to operate in European markets in 2006.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Forbes1-8) StubHub had a positive [cash flow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cash_flow "Cash flow") in 2005 with sales of approximately US$200 million and revenues of approximately US$50 million.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Anthony-9)[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNN1-3) The company successfully lobbied for law changes in New York, Florida, and Pennsylvania regarding ticket sales and the amount above face value for which tickets could be sold.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNN1-3) StubHub made approximately US$6,500 in campaign donations to Florida legislators while lobbying for the law change in that state.[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Ledger2-10)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SFSS1-11) In 2006, the value of tickets sold on the platform was approximately US$400 million and it generated approximately US$100 million in revenue. The company employed approximately 350 people at 12 locations.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CBS1-2)[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNN1-3)

### Acquisition by eBay

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/EBayTorontoOffice2.JPG/500px-EBayTorontoOffice2.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:EBayTorontoOffice2.JPG) eBay, PayPal, Kijiji, and StubHub in Toronto

Fluhr sold StubHub to eBay in 2007 for US$310 million and left the company.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT1-1) [Chris Tsakalakis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chris_Tsakalakis "Chris Tsakalakis") became company president.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SBJ4-12)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-TC2-13) By 2008, StubHub was selling approximately US$5 billion in tickets annually.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Billboard1-14) It had partnerships with 30 college and professional sports teams, including those in the [NFL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NFL "NFL") and [NBA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NBA "NBA"), by the time of eBay's purchase.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Reuters1-15) Some teams took exception to StubHub's business model, with the [New England Patriots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Patriots "New England Patriots") suing the company in November 2006 for allegedly encouraging customers to break Massachusetts law by selling tickets for more than US$2 above their [face value](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Face_value "Face value"). StubHub countersued, saying the Patriots were engaging in unfair trade practices.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CBS1-2)[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-BG1-16) A [Massachusetts Superior Court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Superior_Court "Massachusetts Superior Court") judge in 2007 ordered StubHub to provide the Patriots with a list of 13,000 users who had sold or purchased game tickets.[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ESPN3-17) The suit was settled under undisclosed terms in 2009.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CWB1-18)

In 2006, more than 100 [New York Yankees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Yankees "New York Yankees") season-ticket holders suspected of reselling their regular-season seats on StubHub received letters denying them the right to buy playoff tickets and barring them from buying season tickets for the 2007 season. StubHub criticized the policy as "a witch hunt against us and eBay for giving fans more access to these games".[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-19) The [New York Yankees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Yankees "New York Yankees") revoked the [season tickets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Season_ticket "Season ticket") of those who resold them on StubHub, saying that doing so violated its licensing policy.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CBS1-2)[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT3-20)

[Ticketmaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticketmaster "Ticketmaster") sued StubHub in 2007 for offering "official premium tickets" to events for which Ticketmaster had exclusivity deals. The suit claimed StubHub intentionally interfered with Ticketmaster's venue contracts.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Billboard1-14) StubHub said the tickets were being resold by the management company for the acts, Ticketmaster's fees had been paid, and that StubHub never owned any ticket inventory.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Billboard6-21) The following year, Ticketmaster developed a method of paperless ticketing that made it difficult to transfer or resell tickets, implementing the methodology prior to an [AC/DC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AC/DC "AC/DC") concert in [Detroit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detroit "Detroit"). StubHub opposed the blocking of ticket transfers, saying that it hindered the choices available to purchasers.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Billboard1-14)

[Major League Baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Baseball "Major League Baseball") partnered with StubHub in 2007 to become the league's official online ticket reseller.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-LAT1-22) The agreement between the league and StubHub was renewed in 2012 with modifications, including a minimum price of US$6 for tickets and inclusion of all fees at the time of selecting seats, which StubHub said corrected an "optics issue" caused by fees not being calculated until later in the purchase process. This pricing structure was known as "all-in" pricing, which StubHub made standard across all ticket sales in 2013.[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ESPN1-4) Some teams, including the Yankees and [Los Angeles Angels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels "Los Angeles Angels"), opted out of the renewal.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SBJ3-23) StubHub reversed its all-in pricing in 2015 due to declining sales.[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-WSJ1-24) It had revenues of approximately US$940 million in 2016.[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-FT1-25)

By May 2011, StubHub had 62 partners across entertainment fields, including with the [Fiesta Bowl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiesta_Bowl "Fiesta Bowl"), [Boston Red Sox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox "Boston Red Sox"), and [Ultimate Fighting Championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship "Ultimate Fighting Championship").[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Wong-26) It partnered with [Paciolan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paciolan "Paciolan") in 2011 and in conjunction with that deal had partnered with 23 colleges and universities in the United States.[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-27) StubHub launched a pilot program in the United Kingdom in December 2011 and began full operations in the country in March 2012.[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-FT2-28) [Anschutz Entertainment Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anschutz_Entertainment_Group "Anschutz Entertainment Group") (AEG) and StubHub partnered that year to make StubHub the official ticket reseller for AEG's venues and [AXS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AXS_(company) "AXS (company)"), AEG's ticketing platform.[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Variety1-29) StubHub was criticized by organizers of [12-12-12: The Concert for Sandy Relief](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/12-12-12:_The_Concert_for_Sandy_Relief "12-12-12: The Concert for Sandy Relief") in December 2012 for allowing scalpers to resell tickets to the event for significant markups while proceeds did not go to the relief effort. In response, the company donated its fees for the ticket sales, totaling approximately US$500,000, to the [Robin Hood Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robin_Hood_Foundation "Robin Hood Foundation").[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT2-30) The Yankees sued StubHub for opening an office within 1,500 feet of [Yankee Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yankee_Stadium "Yankee Stadium") in March 2013. The parties settled under undisclosed terms the following month.[\[31\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NBC1-31) StubHub signed a three-year sponsorship deal with [Jockey Club Racecourses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jockey_Club_Racecourses "Jockey Club Racecourses") to sell tickets for events at [Sandown Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandown_Park "Sandown Park"), [Epsom Downs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epsom_Downs_Racecourse "Epsom Downs Racecourse"), and [Kempton Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kempton_Park_Racecourse "Kempton Park Racecourse") in 2013.[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SP1-32) The same year, it purchased the naming rights for the [Home Depot Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_Depot_Center "Home Depot Center"), the stadium of the [LA Galaxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LA_Galaxy "LA Galaxy"), in a six-year deal.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SBJ3-23)

Tsakalakis resigned in November 2014. By that time, company revenue had grown to approximately US$2 billion.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SBJ4-12) He was succeeded by [Scott Cutler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scott_Cutler_(business_executive) "Scott Cutler (business executive)").[\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Fortune2-33) StubHub added [PayPal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PayPal "PayPal") integration for [Android](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Android_(operating_system) "Android (operating system)") users, personalization algorithms, and the ability present a barcode within the app to gain entry to event venues in 2015.[\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-RD1-34) The same year, StubHub sued Ticketmaster and the [Golden State Warriors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors "Golden State Warriors") in an [antitrust](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antitrust "Antitrust") lawsuit that alleged the latter two had conspired to reduce resale of tickets on StubHub's platform by telling ticketholders that reselling through platforms other than Ticketmaster was unsafe. The suit was dismissed in November 2015.[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ESPN5-35)[\[36\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ESPN6-36)

The United Kingdom revised rules for secondary ticket sellers in 2015, requiring that sellers include row and seat numbers. StubHub pledged to improve the information provided to customers.[\[37\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Guardian4-37) The country's [Competition and Markets Authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Competition_and_Markets_Authority "Competition and Markets Authority") (CMA) opened an investigation into four secondary ticket marketplaces the following year: StubHub, Viagogo, [Seatwave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seatwave "Seatwave"), and GetMeIn. The investigation concerned relationships and data sharing between ticket scalpers and the resale platforms.[\[38\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Guardian1-38) Documents released with the [Paradise Papers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paradise_Papers "Paradise Papers") showed Julien Lavallee was using StubHub, [Vivid Seats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vivid_Seats "Vivid Seats") and Ticketmaster to run a multimillion dollar ticket resale business.[\[39\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CBC1-39) According to a November 9, 2017 article published in _The Toronto Star_, Lavallée was able to expand his business using "exploitative tactics" that "gam\[e\] the ticket marketplace and put entertainment beyond the reach of millions of fans who can’t compete with large-scale scalping operations."[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-scooping-40)

Documents in the papers suggested Lavallee and StubHub had a partnership to expand Lavallee's operation to the United Kingdom. Investigations started by the CMA and [Trading Standards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trading_Standards "Trading Standards") as a result of the Paradise Papers showed StubHub offered a separate section of its website for high-volume ticket sellers, with tools that made it easier to batch upload tickets and reduced fees for hitting certain sales volumes. They also showed it likely that Lavellee used [bots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_bot "Internet bot") to purchase large volumes of tickets for resale.[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Star1-41) The CMA raided StubHub's offices in 2017 to acquire information for the investigation and subsequently expanded its scope to include advertising for tickets not yet in stock and [high-pressure sales tactics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hard_sell "Hard sell").[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Guardian2-42) StubHub made a "formal commitment" to improve information provided to consumers in April 2018.[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Guardian6-43) In 2020, the CMA said StubHub was not providing adequate information to customers about venues that may not accept resold tickets, was engaging in high-pressure sales tactics, and was not providing exact locations of seats and addresses of vendors. The company said it was working to resolve "valid concerns" raised by the CMA.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-BBC1-44)

StubHub was the first company to purchase a jersey advertisement patch with one of the [big four](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_professional_sports_leagues_in_the_United_States_and_Canada "Major professional sports leagues in the United States and Canada") American sports leagues, signing a 3-year, US$15 million deal with the [Philadelphia 76ers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers "Philadelphia 76ers") to put a patch on players' jerseys beginning in the [2017-2018 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Philadelphia_76ers_season "2017–18 Philadelphia 76ers season").[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ESPN4-45) StubHub sold US$4.75 billion worth of tickets in 44 countries in 2018.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT1-1) [Sukhinder Singh Cassidy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sukhinder_Singh_Cassidy "Sukhinder Singh Cassidy") was named company president of StubHub that year, replacing Cutler, who became a senior vice president within eBay.[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNET1-46) StubHub argued in favor of mandating "all-in" pricing in the United States during Congressional testimony in 2020.[\[47\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ESPN2-47)

### Acquisition by Viagogo and Eric Baker

In November 2019, it was announced that [Viagogo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viagogo "Viagogo"), the company Baker founded after leaving StubHub, would purchase StubHub for US$4.05 billion.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT1-1) A week prior to the announcement, the [United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_House_Committee_on_Energy_and_Commerce "United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce") announced it would be investigating ticket sales and sellers and requested information from [Live Nation Entertainment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Live_Nation_Entertainment "Live Nation Entertainment") and StubHub on their practices.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-NYT1-1) The CMA opened an investigation into the acquisition. While it was ongoing, the companies were barred from integrating, though the sale could continue.[\[48\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Billboard3-48) Baker completed the sale in February 2020, approximately 5 weeks before the [COVID-19 lockdowns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_lockdowns "COVID-19 lockdowns"). The [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic") led to the company losing approximately 90% of its revenue. Due to its timing with the pandemic, _[Forbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes "Forbes")_'s Noah Kirsch described the transaction as "one of the worst deals in history".[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Forbes1-8)

The purchase by Viagogo was finalized in September 2021. Both were placed under the management of a new company, StubHub Holdings.[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SBJ1-49) As part of the deal, the CMA required StubHub to sell its operations outside North America; the Stubhub International brand was sold to Digital Fuel Capital.[\[50\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-Guardian7-50) Baker became chief executive of the company. It closed its offices in Hong Kong and San Francisco in 2022. Nayaab Islam was named company president that year.[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SBJ2-51) In 2024, [Attorney General for the District of Columbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attorney_General_for_the_District_of_Columbia "Attorney General for the District of Columbia") [Brian Schwalb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Schwalb "Brian Schwalb") filed a lawsuit against StubHub alleging the company was using [drip pricing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drip_pricing "Drip pricing") and a countdown clock to create a false sense of urgency, thereby misleading customers. StubHub said its practices comply with the law, are consistent with the practices of competitors, and that it supported uniform "all-in" pricing regulations.[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-CNN2-52)

StubHub Holdings had revenues of US$1.77 billion in 2024.[\[53\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-53)

StubHub Holdings became a [public company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_company "Public company") via an [initial public offering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Initial_public_offering "Initial public offering") in September 2025.[\[54\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-54) The IPO came after previous delays in 2022 and early 2024.[\[55\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-55)[\[56\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-56)

## Operations

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/StubHub_Storefront_%2848105955247%29.jpg/500px-StubHub_Storefront_%2848105955247%29.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:StubHub_Storefront_(48105955247).jpg) New York City's Walk-In Store

StubHub is an online [ticket resale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticket_resale "Ticket resale") marketplace. The company allows ticket holders to sell their tickets to most events at prices set by the seller. StubHub generates revenue by collecting fees from the buyer and seller of 10 and 15 percent, respectively.[\[57\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-ABC1-57) Payments are handled via PayPal API.

StubHub guarantees delivery to buyers by the time of the event, refunds for late or incorrect tickets, and sends staff to major venues to replace faulty tickets when possible.[\[58\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-DamonWilliams-58)[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-KPF1-59)

The company has offices in Los Angeles, New York City, Taiwan, China, Ireland, and Switzerland.[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub#cite_note-SBJ2-51)

### Partnerships
