SeatGeek

**SeatGeek** is a ticketing platform headquartered in New York City that enables users to [buy and sell tickets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticket_exchange "Ticket exchange") for live sports, concerts, and theater events through its website and [mobile app](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile_app "Mobile app").[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-1) Originally launched as an aggregator of listings on the [secondary ticketing market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticket_resale "Ticket resale"), the company now operates as both a secondary marketplace and a primary ticketing provider for sports teams and live event venues, and has emerged as a competitor to [Ticketmaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticketmaster "Ticketmaster") in selling tickets directly on behalf of teams.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-paulson-2)[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-3)

Its box office clients include several major professional sports teams such as the [Dallas Cowboys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dallas_Cowboys "Dallas Cowboys"), [Arizona Cardinals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arizona_Cardinals "Arizona Cardinals"), [New Orleans Saints](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans_Saints "New Orleans Saints"), [Baltimore Ravens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens "Baltimore Ravens"), [Cleveland Cavaliers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers "Cleveland Cavaliers"), [New Orleans Pelicans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans_Pelicans "New Orleans Pelicans"), [Florida Panthers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida_Panthers "Florida Panthers") and the majority of clubs across the [English Premier League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Premier_League "English Premier League"), including [Liverpool F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liverpool_F.C. "Liverpool F.C.") and [Manchester City F.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C. "Manchester City F.C.")[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-paulson-2)[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-4)[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-6)[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-Young-7)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-8) The platform also handles all of ticketing, including concerts, for large venues like [AT&T Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AT&T_Stadium "AT&T Stadium") and [Rocket Arena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket_Mortgage_FieldHouse "Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse").[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-9)[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-10)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-11) Theater clients include [Jujamcyn Theaters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jujamcyn_Theaters "Jujamcyn Theaters"), which operates five of the 41 [Broadway theatres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadway_theatre "Broadway theatre") in [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "New York City"), and Lloyd Webber Theatre Group in [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "London").[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-paulson-2)[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-12)

## History

### 2009 - 2015: Founding

SeatGeek was founded in 2009 by Russell D’Souza and Jack Groetzinger while participating in DreamIt Ventures, a startup accelerator program in [Philadelphia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philadelphia "Philadelphia"). The company launched publicly in September of that year at the TechCrunch50 conference, where it was recognized by _[VentureBeat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VentureBeat "VentureBeat")_ and [CNET](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CNET "CNET") as one of the top startups of the event.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-13) In May, the company had received $20,000 in seed funding from DreamIT Ventures.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-14) Later, Eric Waller joined as SeatGeek's first employee and third co-founder.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-15)

Initially focused on aggregating ticket listings from the secondary market, SeatGeek raised several rounds of seed and [Series A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Series_A_round "Series A round") financing during 2010 and 2011 to expand its platform.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-16) The company later announced an investment from [Ashton Kutcher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashton_Kutcher "Ashton Kutcher") and [Guy Oseary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guy_Oseary "Guy Oseary")’s [A-Grade Investments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A-Grade_Investments "A-Grade Investments").[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-17) During this early period, SeatGeek's co-founders, D’Souza and Groetzinger, were named among _Bloomberg BusinessWeek_’s “America’s Best Young Entrepreneurs” and featured in _Business Insider_’s “Silicon Alley 100: New York’s Coolest Tech People.”[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-18)[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-19)

In 2013, SeatGeek announced the acquisition of [FanSnap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FanSnap "FanSnap"), a competing ticket search engine. SeatGeek discontinued the FanSnap search engine and rolled it into their existing ticket search platform.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-20) Later that year it announced a partnership with [Telecharge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telecharge "Telecharge"), a Broadway ticketing service.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-21)[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-22)

By 2015, SeatGeek had attracted significant venture backing, including a $62 million Series C funding round led by [Technology Crossover Ventures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology_Crossover_Ventures "Technology Crossover Ventures").[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-23) The investment supported the company’s expansion beyond its original role as a ticket aggregator and positioned it to compete directly with [StubHub](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub "StubHub") as a resale ticket marketplace.[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-24)

### 2016–2017: Entrance into Primary Ticketing

In 2016, SeatGeek entered the primary ticketing market, positioning itself as a competitor to Ticketmaster.[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-25) That July, the company signed a league-wide agreement with [Major League Soccer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Soccer "Major League Soccer") to create an open ticketing network that allowed third-party websites to sell tickets to MLS matches.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-26) As part of the deal, SeatGeek became the official ticketing provider for [Sporting Kansas City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sporting_Kansas_City "Sporting Kansas City"), making the club its first primary box office partner.[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-27)[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-28)

The company expanded its primary ticketing business in 2017. [Seattle Sounders FC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle_Sounders_FC "Seattle Sounders FC") announced that SeatGeek would become its official ticketing partner, and later that year the company was selected as the primary ticketing provider for the [New Orleans Saints](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans_Saints "New Orleans Saints") and [New Orleans Pelicans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans_Pelicans "New Orleans Pelicans").[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-29)[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-30) SeatGeek also acquired Toptix, an Israeli ticketing software company founded by Eli Dagan and Yehuda Yuval, to strengthen its box office technology capabilities.[\[31\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-31)

### 2018–2019: International Expansion and Innovation Awards

In 2018, SeatGeek became a distribution partner of the [National Football League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Football_League "National Football League") (NFL), allowing the company to sell verified tickets through its marketplace.[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-eben-32) A month later, the company announced that it replaced Ticketmaster as the primary box office partner of the [Dallas Cowboys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dallas_Cowboys "Dallas Cowboys").[\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-33) That same year, SeatGeek reached a naming rights agreement with the [Chicago Fire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago_Fire_FC "Chicago Fire FC")’s stadium in [Bridgeview, Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bridgeview,_Illinois "Bridgeview, Illinois"), which was renamed [SeatGeek Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek_Stadium "SeatGeek Stadium").[\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-34) In the United Kingdom, [Premier League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Premier_League "Premier League") club [Manchester City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C. "Manchester City F.C.") selected SeatGeek as its exclusive ticketing provider for both men’s and women’s matches.[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-35) In 2019, it was also appointed the official ticketing partner of [Lord's Cricket Ground](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lord's_Cricket_Ground "Lord's Cricket Ground") in London, marking its entry into the cricket market.[\[36\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-36)

SeatGeek was also named one of [Fast Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_Company "Fast Company")’s “Most Innovative Companies” in the live events category in both 2018 and 2019.[\[37\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-37)[\[38\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-38)

### 2020–2021: Pandemic and Series E Funding

In March 2020, [Liverpool F.C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liverpool_F.C. "Liverpool F.C."). announced a primary ticketing partnership with SeatGeek.[\[39\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-39) The following month, the company faced a class action lawsuit over refunds for events canceled during the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic").[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-40) Plaintiffs alleged that SeatGeek had refused to issue cash refunds, but the company denied wrongdoing, maintaining that it acted in accordance with its published terms of use and had provided customers with the option of receiving cash.[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-41) The case was later settled and closed. Later that year, it introduced “SeatGeek Adapt,” a suite of products designed to support social distancing, timed entry, and mobile concessions ordering at reduced-capacity venues.[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-sbj-42)

In 2021, [Jujamcyn Theaters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jujamcyn_Theaters "Jujamcyn Theaters") selected SeatGeek as its primary ticketing provider, marking the company’s first Broadway partnership.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-paulson-2) Former [Women's National Basketball Association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Women's_National_Basketball_Association "Women's National Basketball Association") president [Laurel J. Richie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurel_J._Richie "Laurel J. Richie") also joined its board of directors.[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-43) That year SeatGeek launched “Rally,” an in-app platform that allowed fans to order concessions, book [rideshares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carpool "Carpool"), and access stadium guides.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-shlomosprung-44) Rally debuted with the [Cleveland Cavaliers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers "Cleveland Cavaliers") at [Rocket Arena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocket_Arena "Rocket Arena") and was later adopted by [Austin FC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austin_FC "Austin FC") at [Q2 Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Q2_Stadium "Q2 Stadium").[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-45) That year the company became the primary ticketing partner of the [Brooklyn Nets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brooklyn_Nets "Brooklyn Nets") and supplied the primary ticketing technology used for all events at [Barclays Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barclays_Center "Barclays Center").[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-46) That fall, it introduced “SeatGeek Swaps,” a feature allowing ticket holders to return tickets for credit up to 72 hours before an event.[\[47\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-47)

In October, SeatGeek announced plans to go public through a [special-purpose acquisition company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special-purpose_acquisition_company "Special-purpose acquisition company") (SPAC) merger valued at $1.35 billion.[\[48\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-48) The deal was later terminated, and in 2022 SeatGeek instead raised $238 million in a Series E funding round led by [Accel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accel_(company) "Accel (company)"), with participation from [Wellington Management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wellington_Management_Company "Wellington Management Company"), [Arctos Partners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctos_Partners "Arctos Partners"), and [Qualtrics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qualtrics "Qualtrics") founder [Ryan Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryan_Smith_(businessman) "Ryan Smith (businessman)"), valuing the company at about $1 billion.[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-49)

### 2022–2024: Partnerships and Product Development

In 2022, SeatGeek added new primary ticketing partners, including the [Washington Commanders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_Commanders "Washington Commanders") and [Baltimore Ravens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens "Baltimore Ravens") of the NFL, the [Utah Jazz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utah_Jazz "Utah Jazz") of the [NBA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NBA "NBA"), and the [Florida Panthers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida_Panthers "Florida Panthers") of the [NHL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NHL "NHL").[\[50\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-50)[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-51)[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-52)[\[53\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-53) That year, the company was also named one of _Fast Company_’s “Most Innovative Companies” in the live events category for the third time, having previously received the recognition in 2018 and 2019. [\[54\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-54)

In January 2023, [Billboard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_(magazine) "Billboard (magazine)") reported that Brooklyn Sports Entertainment Global, the parent of Barclay's Center, ended its partnership with SeatGeek after technical issues led to disappointing concert ticket sales.[\[55\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-55) Ticketmaster resumed ticketing at Barclays Center starting that year. During a [Senate Judiciary Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senate_Judiciary_Committee "Senate Judiciary Committee") hearing on ticketing competition, SeatGeek CEO Jack Groetzinger cited the decision as an example of [Live Nation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Live_Nation_Entertainment "Live Nation Entertainment")’s ability to pressure venues that partnered [Ticketmaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticketmaster "Ticketmaster") competitors.[\[56\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-56)[\[57\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-57) In February 2023, SeatGeek replaced StubHub as the official secondary marketplace for [Paciolan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learfield "Learfield"), the largest ticketing company in college athletics, and integrated its resale platform with Paciolan’s primary ticketing system.[\[58\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-58) That same month, [Major League Baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Baseball "Major League Baseball") named SeatGeek its official ticket marketplace, giving the company partnerships across all major North American sports leagues and again displacing StubHub.[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-59)

The following month, SeatGeek signed a primary ticketing deal with the [Tennessee Titans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tennessee_Titans "Tennessee Titans"), its sixth NFL team, and later that year entered into a tour-wide ticketing partnership with the [LPGA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LPGA "LPGA"), covering all U.S.-based tournaments.[\[60\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-60)[\[61\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-61) SeatGeek also reached an agreement with the [PGA of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Professional_Golfers'_Association_of_America "Professional Golfers' Association of America") to become its official ticketing supplier for championship events including the [PGA Championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PGA_Championship "PGA Championship"), the [KPMG Women’s PGA Championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KPMG_Women's_PGA_Championship "KPMG Women's PGA Championship"), and the [Ryder Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryder_Cup "Ryder Cup").[\[62\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-62)SeatGeek also received workplace recognition during this time. In May 2023, the company was named to _Inc. Magazine_’s list of Best Workplaces for the second consecutive year.

The company continued to expand into new markets in 2024. In September, SeatGeek was announced as the official ticketing partner of the [Utah Mammoth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utah_Mammoth "Utah Mammoth"), the NHL’s newest franchise, which joined existing SeatGeek NHL client the [Florida Panthers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida_Panthers "Florida Panthers").[\[63\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-63) During this period, SeatGeek also introduced several new product features. In February 2024, it launched Next Fan Up, which included an AI-powered “Smart Pricing” tool designed to help fans list and resell tickets more easily.[\[64\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-64) In August, it released Parties, a ticket-sharing feature intended to simplify group attendance at live events.[\[65\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-65) As of October 2024, the [Better Business Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Better_Business_Bureau "Better Business Bureau") gave SeatGeek an “F” rating, with 1,866 consumer complaints filed against the company over the prior three years.[\[66\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-66) At the same time, the company held a 4.5 out of 5 rating on [Trustpilot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trustpilot "Trustpilot") based on more than 14,000 customer reviews.[\[67\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-67)

### 2025 - Present

In 2025, SeatGeek launched Concourse Maps, a venue navigation tool within its Rally platform that allows fans to locate concessions, merchandise, and other amenities.[\[68\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-68) Later that year, it released Beyond the Seat, a feature designed to enhance ticket listings with richer visuals and details of premium experiences.[\[69\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-69)

During the same period, [Premier League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Premier_League "Premier League") club [AFC Bournemouth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AFC_Bournemouth "AFC Bournemouth") announced it would transition to SeatGeek’s ticketing system beginning with the 2025–26 season. The [United States Golf Association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Golf_Association "United States Golf Association") selected the company as the official ticketing provider for its championships, including the [U.S. Open](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._Open_(golf) "U.S. Open (golf)"), U.S. Women’s Open, and U.S. Senior Open, starting in 2026.[\[70\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-70) In [Major League Soccer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Soccer "Major League Soccer"), the [Philadelphia Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philadelphia_Union "Philadelphia Union") entered into a multi-year partnership that will make SeatGeek its official ticketing partner beginning in 2026.[\[71\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-71)

SeatGeek also implemented all-in pricing across its platform, displaying ticket prices inclusive of fees in accordance with new Federal Trade Commission guidelines on price transparency.[\[72\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-72)

FanSnap

**FanSnap** was an online [search engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search_engine "Search engine") for live event tickets, based in [Palo Alto, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California "Palo Alto, California").[\[73\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-73) Founded in 2007, FanSnap aggregated event-level ticket data from event ticket providers. FanSnap's search engine combined results from ticket issuers and various providers in the [ticket resale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ticket_resale "Ticket resale") industry including ticket resale marketplaces such as RazorGator, [StubHub](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/StubHub "StubHub"), TicketNetwork and [eBay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EBay "EBay"), as well as ticket brokers including Ace Ticket, AllShows.com, Barry's Tickets, Gold Coast Tickets and Las Vegas Tickets.[\[74\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-74)[\[75\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-75)

FanSnap did not sell tickets directly to its end-users. Instead, it aggregated event-level ticket data from ticket providers, presented that data in response to user searches and directed users to third-party ticket providers for purchase. FanSnap generated revenue through [cost per click](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost_per_click "Cost per click") and [cost per action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost_per_action "Cost per action") advertising based upon its referral of users to such third-party ticket providers.

FanSnap was acquired by [NexTag](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NexTag "NexTag") in December 2011.[\[76\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-76)

FanSnap was then acquired from Nextag/Wize Commerce by rival ticket search engine SeatGeek in November 2013 for an undisclosed sum. As part of the acquisition, the main FanSnap ticket search engine was discontinued and rolled into SeatGeek's existing ticket search offerings.[\[77\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-77)

## Technology and functioning

Initially launched as a ticket search engine, SeatGeek has grown from an aggregator to a primary ticket seller, instituting a platform that allows artists, sports teams, and others to sell tickets directly through the site.[\[78\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-Most_Innovative_Companies-78) Tickets are sorted using the company's DealScore algorithm which finds the combination of best available price and seat location for a particular event. Historically, SeatGeek provided price forecast information in a similar manner to [Farecast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Farecast "Farecast"), an airline ticket aggregation and forecasting site purchased by [Microsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft "Microsoft") in 2008.[\[79\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-79) SeatGeek has seen success in the mobile space, adopting [Apple Pay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Pay "Apple Pay") to provide seamless purchase and payment.[\[80\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-80)[\[81\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-81)

SeatGeek's app offers customizable widgets and integrations with [Lyft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyft "Lyft"), [Snapchat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snapchat "Snapchat") and other services through its proprietary platform called Rally.[\[82\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-82) Features on Rally include an integration where fans can order mobile food and beverage from their seats, rideshares on Lyft, as well as see venue guides and weather reports before the event.[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeatGeek#cite_note-shlomosprung-44)
